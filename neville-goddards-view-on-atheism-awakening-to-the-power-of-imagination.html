Neville Goddard’s View on Atheism: Awakening to the Power of Imagination

Atheism, in the traditional sense, is the rejection of the existence of God. However, in Neville Goddard's teachings, atheism is viewed as a state of consciousness where individuals fail to recognize the true source of power within themselves—the power of their own imagination. Neville's framework, deeply rooted in the Bible, asserts that God is not an external, separate entity but is the imagination itself, the creative force within every person.

Atheism and the Literal Interpretation of the Bible

Neville believed that a literal interpretation of the Bible often obscures its deeper, symbolic meanings. Many traditional religious teachings focus on God as an external figure, which can create a disconnect between individuals and their own creative power. This externalized view of God can lead to skepticism or disbelief, particularly for those who see no evidence of such a deity in the world around them.

In contrast, Neville’s interpretation of the Bible reveals that the "God" of the scriptures is not an external figure but the imagination, the divine force within. By misunderstanding or literalizing biblical texts, many are led to atheism, failing to see that they themselves hold the key to creation within their own minds.

Imagination: The True Power of God

Neville teaches that imagination is the creative force that shapes our reality. He encourages us to understand that we are the creators of our lives through our imaginal acts. Rather than waiting for an external deity, Neville’s view shows that we have the divine power to manifest our desires through imagination. This understanding transforms our relationship with the world, revealing that the power we seek has always been inside us.

Conclusion

Neville Goddard’s teachings offer a profound shift for those who identify as atheists. By recognizing that imagination is the God within, individuals can stop searching for an external force and instead tap into the divine power that shapes their world. The literal interpretation of the Bible has often obscured this truth, but Neville’s symbolic approach brings the focus back to the creative power within—the true essence of God as imagination.
