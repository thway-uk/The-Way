Key Definitions



This is an initial list to assist a reader in the foundational picturing of symbolism.

God is your “I AM” — seated in imagination. (Exodus 3:14)




Genesis 1:2

“The Spirit of God hovered over the waters” Spirit (consciousness) and soul (subconscious) unite in creative stillness.




Genesis 1:11

“The seed is in itself” Creation begins within imagination — what you assume bears fruit.




Genesis 1:26

“Let Us make man in Our image” Imagination is conscious and subconscious joined. “Man” is who you are aware of being, your perception of self, not a physical man.




Genesis 2:23

“Bone of my bones”

Life and form emanate from imagination — the outer drawn from the inner.




Genesis 2:24

"cleave to his wife"

Begins the symbolic journey of love and union in imagination.




Genesis 4:7

“Sin is crouching at the door… but you must master it.”

Sin = failure to imagine lovingly. Choose honour, not fear.
