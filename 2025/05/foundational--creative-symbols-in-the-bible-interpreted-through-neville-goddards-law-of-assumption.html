Foundational & Creative Symbols in the Bible: Interpreted through Neville Goddard’s Law of Assumption

Neville Goddard’s Law of Assumption teaches that by assuming the feeling of the wish fulfilled and impressing this assumption on the subconscious mind, one can manifest their desires. The following symbols in the Bible reflect various stages and principles in the process of manifestation, illustrating the role of the subconscious, the imagination, and assumption in bringing about change.

1. The Rod/Staff: Power of Assumption

The rod or staff symbolises the power of assumption. It represents the ability to direct your thoughts and beliefs with authority, shaping your reality by asserting your will over external circumstances.

2. Stone: Fixed Belief

The stone represents a fixed belief that has become deeply ingrained in the subconscious mind. When an assumption is solidified, it takes on the immovable quality of a stone, manifesting in your reality as a consistent outcome.

3. Water: Subconscious Mind

In Neville’s teachings, water symbolises the subconscious mind, which is malleable and responsive. It reflects the capacity of the subconscious to take the shape of whatever assumptions and beliefs you impress upon it.

4. Bread: Nourishment of Imagination

Bread symbolises the nourishment of the imagination. Just as bread is sustenance for the body, feeding your imagination with the right assumptions is essential to manifesting your desires. The more you nourish your mind with the right thoughts, the more it will grow.

5. Seed: Desire and Manifestation

The seed represents the initial desire or assumption planted in the subconscious mind. As the seed grows and is nurtured with repeated belief and assumption, it manifests as a tangible outcome in the external world.

6. Oil: Awareness of Creative Power

Oil represents the awareness of your creative power. It symbolises the moment of realisation that your imagination and assumptions hold the key to creating the world around you. This awareness is crucial in manifesting desires.

7. Light: Clarity of Assumption

Light represents clarity in your assumptions. It is the mental illumination that comes when you fully understand that assuming the feeling of your wish fulfilled brings about manifestation. Clarity allows you to focus your energy on the right assumptions.

8. Fire: Passion and Transformation

Fire symbolises the passion and transformation that accompanies a powerful, focused desire. It represents the burning intensity with which you must hold onto your assumptions, fueling your imagination until your desires manifest.

9. The Lamb: Innocence and Purity of Assumption

The lamb symbolises innocence and the purity of assumption. Just as a lamb is pure and untainted, so too must your assumptions be free from doubt. The innocence of a child’s belief can bring about powerful manifestations.

10. The Vine: Connection and Flow

The vine represents the connection between consciousness and the world around you. It symbolises the flow of thoughts and assumptions that feed into your reality. Just as a vine needs to be nurtured to grow, so does your imagination need to be constantly fed with the right beliefs.

11. The Key: Access to Inner Power

The key symbolises access to your inner power. It represents unlocking the door to your subconscious mind, where your assumptions are held and from where your manifested reality springs. With the key, you gain access to the creative power within.

12. The Cup: Receptivity and Acceptance

The cup symbolises receptivity and acceptance. Just as a cup holds liquid, your mind holds the assumptions and beliefs you choose to accept. The more open and receptive you are to new ideas and assumptions, the more you can manifest the desires you hold.

13. The Shield: Protection of Assumptions

The shield symbolises protection of your assumptions and beliefs. It acts as a defense against negative thoughts or doubts that may try to distort or weaken your assumptions. Holding firm to your assumptions protects the creative power of your imagination.

14. The Tree: Growth and Fruition

The tree represents growth and the fruition of your desires. Just as a tree grows from a small seed into something great, so do your assumptions, when nurtured, grow and bear fruit. It symbolises the process of manifestation as a natural and inevitable result of sustained belief.

15. The Door: Opportunity and Transition

The door represents opportunity and transition. It signifies the opening through which new assumptions and beliefs enter into your consciousness, allowing you to move from one state of being to another. The door marks a shift from one experience to the next.

16. The Well: Depth of Subconscious

The well symbolises the depth of the subconscious mind. It represents the rich reservoir of thoughts and beliefs that reside beneath the surface, ready to be tapped into and transformed by your assumptions. Just as you draw water from a well, you can draw upon the creative power of the subconscious.

17. The Altar: Place of Transformation

The altar symbolises a sacred space of transformation. It represents the moment when your thoughts and assumptions are laid down in devotion, with faith that they will manifest. The altar is the place of surrender and deep belief in your creative power.

18. The Mountain: Higher Consciousness and Achievement

The mountain represents higher consciousness and the achievement of desires. Just as a mountain represents a great height to be attained, your assumption and belief must rise to meet the level of your desires in order to manifest them.

19. The Sword: Cutting Through Doubt

The sword symbolises the ability to cut through doubt and negative beliefs. It represents the power of your will to sever limiting thoughts, leaving room for new assumptions to take root and manifest.

20. The Crown: Sovereignty of Mind

The crown represents the sovereignty of your mind and the dominion you have over your own reality. As the crown sits on the head of a king, so too do your assumptions sit upon your mind, ruling over the external world you experience.

21. The Church: Collective Belief and Shared Assumptions

The church symbolises the collective belief or shared assumptions of a group of individuals. It represents a place where collective consciousness can be elevated, and the assumptions of the group can come together to create a shared reality. The church is a powerful symbol of group manifestation, where beliefs are reinforced and amplified by the collective.

22. The Temple: Sacred Space of Inner Transformation

The temple symbolises a sacred space of inner transformation. Just as the temple was a place for divine connection, it represents your inner consciousness, where spiritual and mental growth occurs. The temple reflects the idea that within you lies the divine power to create and manifest through assumption, and your own inner temple must be maintained for the creative process to flow.

Conclusion: Manifesting through Assumption

Each of these symbols represents a different aspect of the manifestation process according to Neville Goddard’s Law of Assumption. The rod and staff guide your assumptions, while the stone signifies fixed beliefs. The water reflects the subconscious mind’s adaptability, and bread nourishes the imagination. Oil awakens your creative power, and light illuminates the path to your desires. Through the seed, you plant your desires, and with fire, you fuel your passion for transformation.

The church and temple also play a significant role, reflecting the power of collective belief and the sacred space of inner transformation. Together, these symbols show how the power of assumption can be used to direct your inner world and manifest your desires.

By understanding and applying these symbols, you can enhance your ability to manifest your desires through the power of assumption. In this process, the imagination plays a central role, and by feeding it with clear, focused beliefs, you shape the world around you.
