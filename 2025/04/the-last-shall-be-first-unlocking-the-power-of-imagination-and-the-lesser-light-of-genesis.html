The 'Last Shall Be First': Unlocking the Power of Imagination and the 'Lesser Light' of Genesis

“So the last shall be first, and the first last…”
— Matthew 20:16

This isn’t about time. It’s not about history. It’s a riddle that reveals the hidden structure of consciousness. Neville Goddard taught that the Bible is not literal but symbolic—a psychological document showing how the unseen world within you becomes the world around you.

What appears to be “first” in life—what is visible, factual, and urgent—is not truly first. And what seems “last”—your imagination, your inner assumptions, your silent states of being—is actually the first cause of everything you experience.

This truth is already encoded in the opening lines of Genesis:

“And God made two great lights; the greater light to rule the day, and the lesser light to rule the night…”
— Genesis 1:16

What appears to describe the sun and moon is, in Neville’s interpretation, a symbolic division within consciousness itself. A division between the declarative and the receptive. The visible and the invisible. The “I Am” and the creative power that shapes it.

The Greater Light: “I AM” – The Source of Being

The greater light symbolises your awareness of being—the origin point of all creation. Before anything is imagined or assumed, you must first be. This pure awareness—“I AM”—is the masculine principle in symbolic terms. It initiates. It declares. It sets the tone.

This greater light “rules the day” because it governs your waking consciousness, the place from which you direct thought, assumption, and identity.

Every affirmation begins here:

  • I AM whole

  • I AM free

  • I AM unloved (yes—even the unwanted beliefs begin here)

Neville taught that this is not ego. This is God within you—your foundational identity, from which all else flows.

The Lesser Light: Imagination – The Creative Feminine

The lesser light is imagination—what Neville often called the creative faculty. It is the feminine principle: receptive, fertile, formative.

It rules the night because it governs the unseen. It shapes what the “I AM” declares. It accepts the impressions of identity and gives them form in the outer world.

Neville writes:

“The subconscious faithfully reproduces whatever is impressed upon it.”
— Neville Goddard

So while “I AM” is the greater light—the essence—imagination is the power that brings it forth into experience. It seems “lesser” only because it works in the invisible, but it is no less divine.

From Light to Flesh: The Evolution of Symbolic Division

The symbolism of the greater and lesser lights is not isolated—it is the first movement in a cascading symbolic structure that runs throughout Scripture.

Genesis begins with light and dark, greater and lesser. But this principle of division continues:

  • Heaven and earth

  • Waters above and below

  • Male and female

“Male and female created He them.” — Genesis 1:27

These are not disconnected ideas. They are repeating patterns that symbolise the relationship between source and expression.
The greater light becomes the male principle. The lesser light, the female.

“The Last Shall Be First”: The Hidden Becomes Visible

So what did Jesus mean by “the last shall be first”?

It’s not about morality or reward. It’s a revelation of the creative order.

What appears “last”—imagination, belief, assumption—is in truth first in power.

And what appears “first”—the physical world, the facts of your life—is really last, the echo of what was once assumed.

This is the entire mechanism of manifestation:

  • You imagine a scene (the unseen “last”)

  • You feel it real

  • It externalises as fact (becoming “first” in visibility)

The seen is always the result of the unseen.

Why You Must Honour the Lesser to Reveal the Greater

This is why Neville constantly instructed his readers to assume the state of the wish fulfilled—to dare to believe what the world has not yet shown.

“An assumption, though false, if persisted in, will harden into fact.”
— Neville Goddard

Your current outer life may appear to be “first” in authority—but it is only the final product of yesterday’s imagination.

And what seems small, internal, quiet—the “last”—is the womb of all creation. When you honour the lesser light (imagination), you allow the greater light (I AM) to shine through it.

Conclusion: A Divine Pattern Hidden in the Beginning

Genesis does not open with physical events—it opens with symbolic structure. The greater and lesser lights are not cosmological—they are psychological. They show how creation happens:

  1. “I AM” (the greater light) declares identity

  2. Imagination (the lesser light) conceives it

  3. Experience (the visible world) reflects it

The first is within. The last is without. But the moment you place faith in the inner world—the last—you cause it to become first in appearance.

This is the law hidden in Genesis, fulfilled in the Gospels, and demonstrated in you.

“Change your conception of yourself and you will automatically change the world in which you live.”
— Neville Goddard
