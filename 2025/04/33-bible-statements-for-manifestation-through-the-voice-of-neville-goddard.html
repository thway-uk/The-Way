33 Bible Statements For Manifestation: Through the Voice of Neville Goddard

The Bible is not a moral instruction manual—it is a code of consciousness. Every divine “I” is your I AM speaking. Every line attributed to God is the voice of your imagination, inviting you to embody a new state.

At the centre of all Scripture stands a simple yet thunderous phrase—God’s name itself:

"I AM THAT I AM"

Exodus 3:14
This is the hidden name of God—consciousness aware of itself. Declare “I AM” followed by anything, and you breathe life into it. That is the law and the promise.

Throughout both Old and New Testaments, this divine truth echoes again and again—calling you to awaken:

"I am with you—do not be afraid."

Isaiah 41:10
The presence of God is your awareness of being. When you feel fear, you’ve left your true dwelling place. Return to the I AM—the only presence that creates—and fear dissolves.

"You are fearfully and wonderfully made."

Psalm 139:14
This is a metaphysical reminder: You are imagination clothed in flesh. Your ability to assume a new state and call it real is the most sacred gift.

"No weapon formed against you shall prosper."

Isaiah 54:17
A weapon is any condition that contradicts your desired state. If you hold your assumption faithfully, all opposition is shadow—form without substance.

"You are more than a conqueror."

Romans 8:37
The conqueror fights; the manifestor imagines. You surpass the world when you no longer react, but create from within.

"I know the plans I have for you..."

Jeremiah 29:11
Desire is the plan. It is not random—it is divine compulsion. Enter into the feeling of the fulfilled plan, and you walk in step with the Creator.

"My grace is sufficient for you."

2 Corinthians 12:9
Grace is the effortless movement of consciousness. Once assumed, the state contains within it all the means of fulfilment. You do not strive—grace carries.

"You are the light of the world."

Matthew 5:14
Your assumptions are the world’s illumination. Change your self-concept, and you change the entire atmosphere around you.

"I have given you authority..."

Luke 10:19
To accept a new state of consciousness is to receive authority. Nothing happens unless you consent. This is power—not over others, but over reality itself.

"You did not choose me—I chose you."

John 15:16
Desire is not initiated by ego—it is revelation. The state itself calls you. When you answer through imagination, you enter your anointed role.

"Be strong and courageous—I am with you."

Joshua 1:9
Strength is staying faithful to the unseen. Courage is persisting in the inner world while the outer still reflects the old. “I Am with you” means: the Creative Power walks as you.

"Let the weak say, I am strong."

Joel 3:10
This is the law of assumption in one verse. You do not speak what you feel—you speak what you choose to be. The spoken word, when believed, becomes flesh.

"As a man thinketh in his heart, so is he."

Proverbs 23:7
The heart is the subconscious. Whatever belief lives there becomes your identity. Change the inner conversations, and you change the life.

"Call those things which be not as though they were."

Romans 4:17
Imagination creates by calling. You don’t wait for evidence—you speak and live as though it is already true. That is the nature of faith.

"All things are possible to him who believes."

Mark 9:23
This is not poetic exaggeration—it is literal metaphysics. Belief is imagination held with conviction. Whatever you accept as true becomes lawful.

"You shall decree a thing, and it shall be established."

Job 22:28
Decree is not begging—it is inner command. Speak it with feeling, and the subconscious receives it as law.

"Out of the abundance of the heart, the mouth speaks."

Luke 6:45
Your speech reveals your state. Listen carefully: your outer words reflect the inner assumptions you live from.

"Guard your heart with all diligence, for out of it spring the issues of life."

Proverbs 4:23
The subconscious does not discriminate. It accepts whatever is impressed upon it. Guard it—not with fear, but with focus. Think only from what you want to become.

"Be transformed by the renewing of your mind."

Romans 12:2
Transformation is not effort—it is identification. Renew the mind by assuming the state of the wish fulfilled. That is rebirth.

"According to your faith be it unto you."

Matthew 9:29
Not as you have hoped, but as you have believed. Imagination plus belief is the seed; the harvest is inevitable.

"The kingdom of God is within you."

Luke 17:21
The kingdom is not a place—it is a state. Every possibility exists within you now. When you imagine a new world, you enter it. And the outer must follow.

Jesus’ Words: The I AM in Action

Jesus did not preach religion—he embodied principle. Every “I AM” on his lips was an invitation to awaken your own.

"Before Abraham was, I AM."

John 8:58
This was not a claim to lineage—it was a revelation of eternal being. The “I AM” precedes all history, all identity, all condition. It is the root of creation, and it is within you.

"According to your word, be it unto you."

Luke 1:38
Mary’s response to the angel is the model of inner consent. She did not strive—she agreed. Creation begins when you inwardly say yes.

"If you abide in me, and my words abide in you, ask whatever you will, and it shall be done for you."

John 15:7
To abide is to dwell in a state. When your inner speech matches your desire, and you remain faithful, the world conforms.

"It is done unto you as you have believed."

Matthew 8:13
Not as you have hoped, but as you have believed. Imagination plus belief is the seed; the harvest is inevitable.

"The words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life."

John 6:63
Words are not sound—they are spirit. The inner word, when fused with feeling, animates the invisible into form.

"He that believes in me, the works that I do shall he do also; and greater works than these shall he do..."

John 14:12
Jesus never called for worship—he invited imitation. The “me” is the state of awakened imagination. Enter that state, and miracles become mundane.

"Your faith has made you whole."

Luke 8:48
Wholeness is not earned—it is accepted. The moment you embody the state of already being healed, restored, or fulfilled, it is done.

"Do you believe I am able to do this?"

Matthew 9:28
The power never left your hands. The question is always: can you accept the state as real now?

"Heaven and earth shall pass away, but my words shall not pass away."

Matthew 24:35
Conditions shift. The outer world crumbles and reforms. But the inner Word—the assumption—remains and manifests in divine timing.

"I came that they may have life, and have it more abundantly."

John 10:10
Life, in its fullest sense, is the awakened use of imagination. Abundance is not a reward—it is the natural result of assuming the God-state.

The Divine Secret Hidden in Plain Sight

All of Scripture circles around this one truth: I AM is God. Every promise, every miracle, every character in the Bible exists to reveal this mystery. You are not meant to worship it—you are meant to use it.

As Neville Goddard said:

"God became man that man may become God."

So stop waiting for the Word to come to pass. Speak it. Assume it. Embody it.
That is how the Word becomes flesh—in you.
