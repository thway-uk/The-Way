Joseph's: Coat Of Many Colours

In the rich tapestry of the Bible, Genesis 37 stands out as a profound allegory, shedding light on the relationship between imagination, resistance, and manifestation. Through the story of Joseph and his brothers, we gain a deeper understanding of the role of imagination in shaping reality. Interpreted through the lens of Neville Goddard’s teachings, this chapter becomes a powerful narrative about how imagination, often met with opposition, eventually transforms our external world.

Joseph: The Symbol of Imagination

Joseph, the son of Jacob and Rachel, represents the imagination in its highest form. He is the beloved son, favoured above all his brothers, much as imagination is the most powerful and treasured aspect of consciousness. In Neville’s framework, Joseph’s status as Jacob’s favoured son mirrors how imagination is the "favoured" tool of creation within us. The coat of many colours that Joseph wears is not merely a garment, but a symbol of the limitless potential of imagination. Just as his coat is multi-coloured, so too is imagination capable of creating infinite possibilities in our lives when we harness its power.

The Jealousy of the Brothers: Resistance to Imagination

The jealousy of Joseph’s brothers reveals a powerful truth about the resistance we often face when it comes to embracing the imagination. The brothers—representing the conscious, rational mind—resent Joseph’s dreams and the power that imagination embodies. Their jealousy reflects how our logical, conditioned thinking can feel threatened by the freedom and creativity of imagination. They plot to silence Joseph, much like the rational mind seeks to suppress or restrict the imaginative, creative faculties within us. This resistance, however, only highlights the transformational potential of imagination.

Joseph’s Dream: Imagination Shaping Reality

Joseph’s dreams are central to this story, and in Neville’s interpretation, they represent the incredible power of imagination to shape and create reality. In his dream, Joseph sees his brothers’ sheaves of grain bowing down to his sheaf. This dream isn’t just a vision—it is an insight into the creative process. Through imagination, Joseph sees himself in a position of power, with the world (his brothers, representing external circumstances) submitting to his vision. Neville would assert that this dream is a symbol of how imagination first presents itself as a thought or desire, which then begins to take form in the external world.

It’s a reminder that our imagined reality is the first step towards manifestation. What we envision in our inner world—our thoughts, feelings, and beliefs—will inevitably shape our external reality.

The Sale into Slavery: Suppression of Imagination

Joseph’s brothers, in their jealousy, sell him into slavery, an event that Neville interprets as the rejection or suppression of imagination by the conscious mind. This act of betrayal represents the way the rational mind seeks to confine or control the imagination, often dismissing it as impractical or irrelevant. However, just as Joseph’s eventual rise to power shows, even when imagination is suppressed, it cannot be permanently contained. The momentary suppression of imagination only sets the stage for its eventual triumph.

The Coat of Many Colours: The Misrepresentation of Truth

The brothers’ deception, when they show Jacob the bloody coat and falsely claim Joseph has died, mirrors the distortion of truth by the rational mind. The coat—an outward manifestation of Joseph’s identity—becomes a symbol of how external circumstances often misrepresent the inner truth of our being. The brothers' actions remind us that when imagination is misunderstood or misrepresented, it can lead to a false narrative being imposed on our lives.

Yet, this deception does not change the truth of Joseph’s eventual rise. Just as Joseph’s true nature cannot be hidden, the creative power of imagination cannot be overshadowed for long. It is destined to reveal itself in all its glory.

Conclusion: Imagination Triumphs

Genesis 37, when interpreted through Neville Goddard’s teachings, becomes a narrative of hope and empowerment. It shows us that while imagination—our inner creative power—may face resistance and suppression from the rational mind, it is destined to prevail. Joseph’s story is a reminder that the more we embrace the power of imagination, the more we align ourselves with our creative potential, transforming our outer world in the process.

Imagination, like Joseph, is often misunderstood or rejected. But it is through the trials, the suppression, and the misrepresentation that it ultimately rises to fulfil its true purpose: to shape reality according to our deepest desires and aspirations. Trust in the power of your imagination, for it is the key to unlocking the life you truly desire.
