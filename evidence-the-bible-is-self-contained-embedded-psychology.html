Evidence the Bible is Self-Contained Embedded Psychology

The Bible is not a record of historical events—it is a coded psychological manual. Its symbolic structure is too intricate and consistent across books, genres, centuries, and authors to be accidental. The Mather's table of Hebrew alphabet and number Correspondences is integral to understanding scripture. Scripture’s narrative guides us back to the mind’s original paradise, teaching through rich symbolism.

The Number 40: The Interval of Transformation

This number appears repeatedly throughout the Law, Prophets, and Gospels, always marking a threshold between an old and new state of consciousness:

  • Noah’s flood lasted 40 days (Genesis 7:12)

  • Moses spent 40 years in Egypt, 40 years in Midian, and 40 years leading Israel

  • The Israelites wandered 40 years in the wilderness (Numbers 14:33–34)

  • Elijah fasted for 40 days on Mount Horeb (1 Kings 19:8)

  • Jesus fasted for 40 days (Matthew 4:2)

The Number 12: Cosmic Order and Divine Governance

Twelve recurs as a symbol of divine structure and wholeness:

  • The 12 sons of Jacob became the 12 tribes of Israel (Genesis 49)

  • The high priest’s breastplate bore 12 stones (Exodus 28:21)

  • Jesus chose 12 disciples (Matthew 10:2–4)

  • The New Jerusalem has 12 foundations, gates, and fruits (Revelation 21–22)

A key figure is 144,000, or 12 × 12 × 1,000, signalling the fullness of divine completeness.

Transformation Marked by Name Changes

Throughout Scripture, change of name signals transformation of identity:

  • Abram → Abraham

  • Sarai → Sarah

  • Jacob → Israel

  • Simon → Peter

  • Saul → Paul

Identity here is about inner realignment, not genealogy or geography.

The Pattern of the Barren Womb

Life emerges from apparent barrenness, symbolising hope where there seems none:

  • Sarah (Genesis 18)

  • Rebekah (25:21)

  • Rachel (30:1–2)

  • Hannah (1 Samuel 1)

  • Elizabeth (Luke 1)

The Younger Shall Rule the Elder: Inverted Natural Order

This recurring motif illustrates that spiritual progress often overturns expected norms:

  • Jacob over Esau (Genesis 25:23)

  • Joseph over his older brothers

  • David over his siblings (1 Samuel 16)

  • Jesus teaches: “The last shall be first” (Matthew 20:16)

Three-Day Cycles of Death and Resurrection

Key transitions follow a “third day” pattern of descent and rebirth:

  • Abraham and Isaac’s journey: “On the third day…” (Genesis 22:4)

  • Jonah in the fish (Jonah 1:17)

  • Jesus’s resurrection (Luke 24:7)

Prophets vs Kings: Inner Voice vs Outer Rule

The inner spiritual guide consistently confronts external worldly power:

  • Moses vs Pharaoh

  • Elijah vs Ahab

  • Isaiah vs Uzziah

  • Nathan vs David

  • Jesus vs Herod and Caesar

Major Events Happen on Mountains

Mountains symbolise elevated states of awareness:

  • Abraham on Mount Moriah

  • Moses on Mount Sinai

  • Elijah on Mount Carmel

  • Jesus on the Mount of Olives and the Sermon on the Mount

Water: The Symbol of the Subconscious

Water represents the boundary and flow of inner emotional and spiritual life:

  • Spirit moving on the waters (Genesis 1:2)

  • Rivers flowing from Eden (Genesis 2:10–14)

  • Crossing the Red Sea = crossing subconscious boundaries (Exodus 14)

  • Moses striking the rock releasing water

  • Jordan River as the entry to the promised land

  • Jesus walking on water shows command over the subconscious

Sacrifice and Substitution

The theme of dying to the old self to give birth to the new runs throughout:

  • Ram substituted for Isaac (Genesis 22)

  • Scapegoat for sins (Leviticus 16)

  • Jesus as the sacrificial Lamb (John 1:29)

The Temple as Symbol of Body and Consciousness

The temple’s architecture reflects inner spiritual construction:

  • Built with exact measurements (1 Kings 6)

  • Veil tears at Jesus’s death (Matthew 27:51)

  • “Your body is the temple of God” (1 Corinthians 6:19)

God Appears in Visions and Inner States

Divine encounters happen in altered inner awareness:

  • Abraham’s deep sleep (Genesis 15:12)

  • Jacob’s dream of the ladder (Genesis 28:12)

  • Ezekiel’s visions

  • Daniel’s dreams

  • John’s Revelation (Revelation 1:10)

Cycles Repeat Across Scripture

From Exodus to exile, from Fall to resurrection, cycles represent spiritual evolution:

  • Exodus → Babylonian exile → “Come out of her” (Revelation 18:4)

  • The universal cycle of Fall, Promise, Death, Resurrection, and Ascension

Time in the Bible Is Symbolic

Time communicates inner process rather than chronological fact:

  • “In the beginning…” (Genesis 1:1) vs “Before Abraham was, I Am” (John 8:58)

  • “1,000 years are like a day” (2 Peter 3:8)

  • “There shall be no more time” (Revelation 10:6)

The Number 7: Completion and Divine Order

Seven signals sacred completion and spiritual fullness:

  • Creation completed in 7 days (Genesis 1)

  • Seven lamps on the menorah (Exodus 25:37)

  • Seven seals, trumpets, and bowls in Revelation

  • Seven washes for lepers (Leviticus 14:7)

  • The Sabbath as the 7th day of rest (Exodus 20:8–11)

The Number 4: Earthly Completeness and Stability

Four relates to the physical world and cosmic order:

  • Four directions: North, South, East, West (Ezekiel 37:9–10)

  • Four rivers of Eden (Genesis 2:10–14)

  • Four living creatures around God’s throne (Ezekiel 1; Revelation 4)

  • The four gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John)

  • The earth divided into four parts (Daniel 7:2)

The Song of Solomon: Inner and Outer Union

This poetic book symbolises the union of inner and outer self—the soul’s reunion with the divine. It's language and allegory underpins the entire narrative of the Bible.

Twin Brothers: Inner and Outer Man

Cain and Abel, Jacob and Esau, symbolise the tension and reconciliation between worldly and spiritual self.

Conclusion: Intentional Symbolism, Not Chance

These patterns span different authors, eras, and genres—from Law through Prophets, Writings, Gospels, and Revelation. The consistency is psychological and symbolic—because the Bible is a coded map of the inner man. As Neville Goddard taught:

“The Bible is not secular history. It is the biography of every man.”
