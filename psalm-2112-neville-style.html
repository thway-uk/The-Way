Psalm 21:12: Neville Style

In the world of Neville Goddard’s teachings, the Bible is far more than a collection of ancient texts — it’s a psychological blueprint for understanding the inner workings of the mind and the power of imagination. Each verse is not just a historical account but a metaphysical tool for transforming your reality. Today, we’ll explore Psalm 21:12 in the BBE (Bible in Basic English) translation and interpret it through Neville Goddard’s lens, all while reflecting on the concept of "missing the mark" (or sin).

Psalm 21:12 – BBE Translation

The verse from Psalm 21:12 in the BBE reads:

"For you will make them turn their backs; you will make ready your bows against their faces."

On the surface, this may seem like a poetic description of battle and triumph. But when viewed through Neville Goddard’s teachings, it takes on a deeper, symbolic meaning related to mental focus, imagination, and the power of directed thought.

The Metaphysical Interpretation of Psalm 21:12

In Neville Goddard’s view, the Bible is a psychological map, with each figure, event, and story representing aspects of the human mind. When we look at Psalm 21:12, we see a profound metaphor for the power of our inner imagination in shaping external circumstances.

The phrase “you will make them turn their backs” suggests that opposition or resistance — represented by “them” — will be neutralised or turned away. In Neville's teachings, this can be interpreted as a shift in your internal state of consciousness. By consciously focusing your thoughts, feelings, and imagination on your desired outcome, you can cause any external challenges or negativity to be reversed. Those things that previously appeared as obstacles will, quite literally, turn their backs and cease to stand in your way.

Meanwhile, “you will make ready your bows against their faces” can be understood as preparing your mental energy (the "bows") and aiming it directly at your goal or desire. The “bows” here are symbols of your directed thoughts — your imagination harnessed and focused with intention. The “face” of the opposition represents the visible circumstances in your life that may seem to stand in your way. Yet, when you align your inner vision with your desires, those visible challenges fade or are overcome, just like the force of an arrow hitting its intended target.

"Missing the Mark" — A Metaphysical Perspective on Sin

In traditional religious contexts, “sin” is often understood as moral wrongdoing. However, in Neville Goddard’s interpretation, sin is not about ethical failings but about "missing the mark" — failing to direct your imagination and thoughts correctly to manifest your desires.

Think of it this way: “making ready your bows” in Psalm 21:12 refers to preparing your thoughts with intent and focus. If your thoughts are scattered or misdirected — if you "miss the mark" — then your efforts won’t produce the desired results. This is what Neville considered as “sin” in the psychological sense: when your inner vision is out of alignment with your desired outcome. Your arrows (thoughts) may hit the wrong target, leading to outcomes that are far from what you truly want.

To avoid "missing the mark," Neville Goddard advised a disciplined approach to the imagination. Instead of allowing negative thoughts, doubts, or distractions to take control, you must consistently focus on the mental image of your desired outcome. You must “aim” your thoughts with clarity and precision, trusting that, as you hold this vision firmly in your mind, external circumstances will shift to reflect it.

The Power of Imagination

Psalm 21:12, when interpreted through Neville Goddard’s teachings, highlights the power of imagination and the importance of directed thought in manifesting your desires. It reminds us that our internal state — the thoughts and images we cultivate within our minds — directly impacts our external world. When we focus on our desires with clarity and intention, the obstacles that once seemed insurmountable will disappear.

By taking control of your imagination and aligning your thoughts with your desires, you not only overcome opposition but actively change the course of your life. As you make ready your mental "bows" and aim them at your goals, you prepare yourself to achieve what you once thought impossible. And, by avoiding the temptation to “miss the mark” through negative thinking, you stay on course to manifest the reality you truly desire.

Conclusion

In essence, Psalm 21:12 is a call to action for anyone seeking to master their mind and manifest their desires. It reminds us that our imagination is a powerful tool capable of overcoming any obstacle when directed with clarity and focus. By understanding the metaphysical meaning behind the verse, we can apply Neville Goddard’s teachings to our own lives, ensuring that we are not “missing the mark” but hitting our targets with precision.

The path to manifesting your desires is through mastering the art of focused thought — preparing your mind like a bow ready to strike the target. When you do this, the world around you will shift to reflect your inner vision, and the obstacles that once stood in your way will turn their backs in defeat.
